Q: "What's your story behind becoming an artist/creator?"
A: Started drawing in fourth grade with little alien creatures- made a whole exozoology that was pretty cool. In my freshman year I started an Instagram with ink and pencil doodles. A few years later I started doing found footage. Instagram kind of blew up- I started making games- realized I wasn't being creatively challenged- ditched found footage- got invested in surrealism.
Q: "How did you find your own aesthetics? What fascinates you about it, if anything?"
A: Found a lot of my aesthetic through old media- old anatomy textbooks and games.
Something I both like and would like to develop about my 'aesthetic', is the feeling you've stumbled upon something that's existed for a long time, but you're only now seeing it.
Then again, I feel like sometimes I have to reel myself back in because things have gotten too indecipherable, which isn't ideal.
